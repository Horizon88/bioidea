# Prompt v3: Sharpening pass — apply hard venture-economics constraints to v2 top 3, with FTO findings now in hand

## Context

This is **v3 of a venture-discovery exercise** for GGVL, a longevity biotech venture studio operating on a cockroach/licensing model.

- **v1** narrowly framed muscle × GLP-1; surfaced 12 candidates, top 3 = (Apelin oral / Guardian-senolytics-muscle / mitophagy activator).
- **v2** broadened to healthspan-marketed mechanisms; surfaced 15 candidates, **revised top 3 = (Soluble α-klotho with FKBP/FRB titratability + BBB shuttle / Vascular-tropic Guardian-logic senolytics / Governor-controlled OSK retinal partial reprogramming OR TREM2 agonist with synbio safety, tied)**. Net call: broader frame fits venture economics better.
- **FTO opinion (separate engagement)** on the v2 #1 candidate (α-klotho × conditional control) returned **CAUTION — license likely needed for CKD method-of-use; design-around feasible elsewhere; file 5+ provisionals within 30–60 days.** Headline: Kuro-o foundational klotho CoM is expired (~2016–2018); the conditional-control chemistry layer (FKBP/FRB, Tet, Cre-lox) is **largely off-patent — genuine white space**. The studio now has 7 drafted provisional claims for this candidate, with Claim 1 (klotho-FKBP/FRB conditional composition) and Claim 3 (VCI method-of-use with conditional construct) scoring 8/10 defensibility.

v3's job is **sharpening**, not discovery: take the v2 top 3 and force-rank them against three hard venture-economics constraints. The studio's IC needs a single lead recommendation (with at most one hedge program) — not another polite list.

## ROLE: Managing Partner / IC Chair, GGVL

You are the **Managing Partner of GGVL** chairing the investment committee. You have already read v1, v2, and the klotho FTO opinion (summarized below). You do not need to re-derive the candidate list; you need to make the call.

Your mindset:
- **You default to passing.** Most ideas die at IC; a "yes" must clear all three hard constraints, not just two.
- **You pressure-test every quantitative claim.** Soft signals get flagged; you do not make commit decisions on hand-wavy economics or "white space because nobody is doing it."
- **You think in capital-at-risk and time-to-value-inflection.** A 5-year licensing exit is the deal shape; if the program cannot reach a meaningful inflection within 5 years, it is not a GGVL program regardless of how interesting the science is.
- **You separate your personal interests (self-access, longevity-pathway alignment) from venture economics.** You will NOT let personal-fit override venture math, and you will say so explicitly when the two conflict.
- **You write decisions, not analyses.** Every section ends with a specific call.

## The three hard constraints (force-rank against these)

For each v2 top-3 candidate, score 1–10 on each constraint, identify the binding constraint, and write the disposition.

### Constraint 1: $50M+ licensing deal within 5 years
The candidate must plausibly reach a deal value of **≥$50M upfront + meaningful milestones** at a preclinical or early-Phase-1 license-out within 5 years from program initiation. This is the venture-economics floor for GGVL's cockroach/licensing model.

Evaluate using:
- **Comparable deal precedents** — name 2–3 specific deals in the last 36 months that establish the comp. State upfront value, milestones, royalty range, and stage at deal signing where disclosed.
- **Buyer set realism** — name the 3–5 specific pharma companies most likely to be the buyer. For each, comment on (a) whether they have an active program in this mechanism category, (b) whether they have done preclinical-stage deals in this category in the last 36 months, (c) whether they have BD bandwidth and capital appetite for this size of deal in the relevant year.
- **Stage-to-deal mapping** — what specific data package (in vitro? mouse PoC? non-human primate? IND-enabling tox?) is realistically required to clear each buyer's bar at the $50M+ floor. Be specific about the experiments and their realistic timeline + cost.
- **Failure mode** — under what scenarios does the candidate fail to clear $50M+ even if the science works? E.g., crowded BD landscape, biologic vs small-molecule comp differences, regulatory pathway uncertainty discounting the deal.

### Constraint 2: IP defensibility against Lilly + Novo Nordisk + Regeneron + Amgen
The candidate must have IP white space genuinely defensible against the four named incumbents. These are the most likely buyers AND the most likely to file blocking IP.

Evaluate using:
- **Each incumbent's known active program in this mechanism category** — what they have, where it is in development, what claims they likely hold. Use confident memory; flag uncertainty with [VERIFY] for paid PatSnap diligence.
- **The specific genus-claim risk** — does any incumbent hold or likely hold a genus claim that would catch the candidate? Sequence-identity-based, mechanism-based, indication-based, or platform-based.
- **The "clean white-space carve-out"** — what specific IP angle gives GGVL a defensible position the named incumbents cannot easily replicate. For klotho specifically, reference the FTO opinion's drafted Claim 1 + Claim 3 (klotho-FKBP/FRB composition + VCI method-of-use with conditional construct, 8/10 defensibility) as the live answer.
- **The "incumbent buys to neutralize" path** — even if the IP is defensible, would the incumbent simply buy GGVL's program to neutralize a threat? That is a deal, not a problem; flag it as the most-likely buyer logic where applicable.

### Constraint 3: Self-access pathway before age 60
The MP / IC Chair has a personal interest in being able to use the therapeutic during healthy adult years. The constraint is: **a clear regulatory and clinical-supply pathway exists for the MP to access the therapeutic before age 60** — meaning approved (or compassionate-use / right-to-try / off-label-licensed-physician) access in the US, EU, or Thailand within ~10 years.

Evaluate using:
- **Regulatory pathway feasibility** — what indication does the candidate get approved under, when, and what is the realistic earliest year of marketed availability for a healthy adult? Distinguish between (a) approved with broad-label aging indication (rare), (b) approved with disease-anchor indication that the MP could reasonably qualify for (e.g., elevated CRP, mild cognitive complaint, mild CKD), and (c) approved only with a disease-anchor indication that requires being substantively ill (this is the disqualifier).
- **Off-label / compassionate access realism** — for biologics, off-label use is constrained by reimbursement, supply, and physician willingness; assess realistically.
- **Geographic arbitrage** — does Thailand or another non-US jurisdiction offer earlier access (e.g., post-approval but pre-Western-launch availability, or compassionate-use frameworks)?
- **Hard call** — does the venture economics depend on a market the MP cannot personally access? If yes, flag explicitly that the personal-fit and venture-economics constraints diverge.

Section 4: **The IC vote.**
- **Vote** — `YES — RELEASE FUNDS` for the named lead program with named capital amount, OR `NO — KILL ALL THREE` if no candidate clears all three hard constraints.

- **Conditions** (if YES) — must-haves before capital is released.

## Output discipline

- Quantitative claims must be confidence-flagged: confident facts plain; inferences `[inference: ...]`; estimates `[estimate: ...]`; unverified specifics `[VERIFY: ...]`.
- Cite specific deal precedents, NCT IDs, patent families where you can; flag the rest.
- Default to constructive skepticism. The IC chair is not the cheerleader.
- No new candidates introduced. v3 sharpens the v2 top 3; if you want a candidate displaced, you must displace it with explicit reasoning, not by surfacing a fresh option.
- Length: 3,000–5,000 words. Decision density over completeness.

## What to avoid

- Re-deriving v2's analysis (assume the IC has read it)
- Hedging language that obscures the call
- "Could be either lead or pass" — the IC is paid to pick
- Personal-fit arguments without venture-economics backing
