## 1. Executive Verdict

**CAUTION — Licensing likely required for one narrow CoM family; broad design-around is feasible everywhere else.**

In short: the **Kuro-o foundational klotho composition family** is, on my best read, **expired or near-expired** [estimate: priority 1996–1997 → US composition CoM expired ≈ 2016–2018], so wild-type α-klotho protein composition claims are not the threat. The real risks are (a) **method-of-treatment** claims (CKD especially; cognition is more open) held by UTSW, Klotho Therapeutics/Klotho Inc. successor entities, and 1–2 academic labs; (b) **genus-by-sequence-identity** language in late-2010s/2020s filings that could sweep our ML-evolved variants; and (c) the **silent-killer risk** of a pending application at the α-klotho × conditional-control intersection that has not yet published. The conditional-control chemistry layer (FKBP/FRB, Tet, Cre-lox) is **largely off-patent at the foundational level** — that is genuine white space.

**Bottom line for the IC:** This is a **fileable, defensible program** if (i) provisionals go in within 30–60 days at the **klotho × conditional-control intersection** before any wet-lab disclosure, and (ii) we accept the high probability of needing one method-of-use license in CKD (not VCI). Commit recommended.
